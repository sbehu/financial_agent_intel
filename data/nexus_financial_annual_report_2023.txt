RISK FACTORS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. INTEREST RATE RISK
   Approximately 40% of our loan portfolio is tied to variable rates. A 200bps
   increase in benchmark rates would reduce net interest income by an estimated
   $186 million annually. We employ interest rate swaps covering $1.86 billion
   of notional exposure as a partial hedge.

2. CREDIT RISK & LOAN PORTFOLIO QUALITY
   Non-performing loans represent 1.8% of the total portfolio (industry avg: 2.1%).
   We maintain a credit loss allowance of $744 million (3.2x NPL coverage ratio).
   Concentration in commercial real estate (28% of portfolio) warrants monitoring
   given current market conditions.

3. REGULATORY & COMPLIANCE RISK
   Pending Basel IV implementation in 2025 may require additional Tier 1 capital
   buffers of $620–$930 million. SEC proposed rules on cybersecurity disclosure and
   AI model governance could increase compliance costs by $40–80 million annually.

4. LIQUIDITY RISK
   Our liquidity coverage ratio (LCR) stands at 142%, well above the 100% regulatory
   minimum. Net stable funding ratio (NSFR) is 118%. However, rapid deposit outflows
   in a stress scenario remain a tail risk we continuously monitor.

5. OPERATIONAL & TECHNOLOGY RISK
   Our increasing dependence on AI and machine learning models introduces model risk.
   A significant model failure could impact $3.7 billion of algorithmically-managed
   assets. We maintain comprehensive model validation and governance frameworks.

6. CYBERSECURITY RISK
   We experienced two significant cyber incidents in 2023, neither of which resulted
   in material data loss. Annual cybersecurity spend is $248 million. A successful
   breach could result in regulatory penalties, litigation, and reputational damage.

7. GEOPOLITICAL & MACRO RISK
   With 22% of revenue derived from international operations, adverse geopolitical
   developments — particularly in EMEA — could reduce revenue by up to $496 million
   in a severe scenario.

MANAGEMENT OUTLOOK
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

For FY2024, management guides for:
  • Revenue growth of 10–13% ($13.6–14.0 billion)
  • EBITDA margin expansion of 50–100bps (23.5–24.0%)
  • EPS of $10.00–$10.50 (diluted)
  • Capital expenditure of $450–500 million
  • Share repurchase programme of up to $620 million

The Board has approved a quarterly dividend of $0.38 per share
($1.52 annualised), a 10% increase from FY2023.
